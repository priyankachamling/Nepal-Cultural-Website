Festivals

Although Nepal is multi religious, multi-cultural and multi-linguistic country with different festival including with dashain, Tihar, lhosar, chhath etc. As per the lunar calendar shows the date and time for the festival month. Festival plays important role in our society. It had been followed from the ancestors. Gathering of family, groups etc. are done in this festive month. School, colleges, offices remains close during this season.

  1. Dashain
  2. Tihar
  3. Teej
  4. Holi
  5. Chhath
  6. Sakela
  7. Buddha Jyanti
  8. Losar

Dashain

This is one of the main festival for Hinduism. It occurs at September and October month. People clean the house during this time. The first day of this festival called ghatasthapana in this day they worship the god put jamara visit different temples and (nauratha) occurs. During this 8days they worship different gods, on the ninth day they worship and put tika to kumari keti. On the last day they wear new dresses put tika from elder take blessing. They eat delicious food, meat etc.

Tihar

This is known as second most important festival which we celebrated in 5 days. Within this 5 days we worship crow, dog, cow and ox. The very first day is known as (kagh tihar) we worship crow give them food. The second day is known as kukur tihar in this day we give food to dog worship them. The third day is known as (laxmi puja) the day when women keeps fasting cleans the house and welcome laxmi in our house with different colorful lights play vailo. The fourth day which we worship ox in this day give them food and play deusi. The last day vaitika we put seven different colors to our brothers give them sweets and take gifts.

Teej

This is the festival which falls in July and august. It is special festival for women before the festival they celebrated in midnight as named (dar) to take fast in next day. Married women take fast for their husband happiness and prosperous life. Unmarried girls take fast to get good husband. They wear sari, jeweler’s and go to the temple and dance with the group.

Holi

We celebrate this day in the month between February and March. People play with colors and water. Most of the people play in different places with their family and friends and also with their society. They celebrate by dancing playing music. The second day is celebrated in terai region.

Chhath

Chhath parva is celebrated by natives of Mithila region of Nepal and India. This festival is dedicated to god Sun. Sun is called Surya in Sanscrit, Chhat parba is thanks giving to lord Surya for sustaining life on earth and to worship him to provide his continuous blessings to mankind.The Sun, source power of nature, considered the god of energy and lord of the life-force, is worshiped in Chhath to promote well-being, prosperity and progress. Hindu people believe sun helps to cure many illness and diseases. Sun is considered as source of healing too. Sun is also considered as token of stability and prosperity. Therefore, people worship sun to ensure the longevity and prosperity of family members, friends and beloved ones.

Sakela

Sakela is the main festival of kiratis people which is celebrated twice a year and is distinguished by two names Ubhauli and Udhauli. Sakela Ubhauli is celebrated during Baisakh Purnima and Sakela Udhauli is celebrated during the full moon day in the month of Mangsir.

Buddha Jyanti

Buddha Jayanti is a special day for both Hindus and Buddhists in Nepal. On this day people celebrate the life of Lord Buddha; his Birth, Enlightenment and Mahaparinirvana (Death). Lumbini, in the western Terai plains of Nepal, is the place where Prince Siddhartha (known as the Buddha) was born. The newly-born Prince is believed to have taken seven steps and uttered a timeless message to all humanity. It is believed that this happned in the beautiful Sal grove, which is now the focal point of the Lumbini Garden area.

Losar

Losar is one of the main festivals in Nepal. The festival is celebrated in Buddhist religious follower communities like Tamang, Sherpa, Gurung, and Thakali. who live in the hilly and mountainous region. The literal meaning of Losar is New Year and follows the cycle of 12 years called Lohokor. Barga (Lo)is the special name given to each year and represented by different animals with its reputed attributes like mouse, cow, tiger, cat, garuda, serpent, horse, sheep, monkey, bird, dog, and deer. The cycle of animals is arranged in a single circle printed on paper or cloth. In Nepal when there was no calendar, the 12 rotation system was used to calculate peoples’ ages.

Shivaratri

This is the festival which falls in the month of February and March. In this day people go to Shiva temples and mostly in the pashupatinath. It is the day to interact with different sadhus and babas. It is the day for the men. Government provide different foods and accommodation to those people who seat nearby temple. It is known as the great night of god Shiva. In this day people sit late night eat delicious food like haluwa with bhang.
