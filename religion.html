Religion

Religion in Nepal is one of the country's big highlights at the moment. The situation in Nepal has changed quite a bit due to the dramatic changes made in the field of religion. Nepal is a nation that is multi-ethnic, multi-cultural and multi-religious. It was historically a Hindu republic, but things have changed following the declaration of secularism. Now it is a sanctuary for people of all religions.. With 81.34% of the population, the most prevalent religion here remains Hinduism, and with 9% becoming the second dominant, Buddhism trails behind. The Declaration of Secularism caused the number of adherents of other religions to increase. It acted as a bridge to go out into the world of diversity from the previous Hindu cultures. While secularism enables individuals to practice a religion, it is strictly forbidden to control or compel others.List of Religions in Nepal:

  1. Hinduism
  2. Buddhism
  3. Islam
  4. Kiratism
  5. Christianity

Hinduism

Hinduism is the religion that is most dominant in Nepal. It is said that Hinduism existed prior to human civilization. It is the oldest religion in Nepal, introduced by the first rulers. Most of Nepal's ruling dynasties were Hindu, so Hinduism is well developed and widespread among this nation's inhabitants. Thus in Nepal, Hinduism was founded as the main religion. Festivals like Dashain, Tihar, Gai Jatra and Holi are celebrated with great enthusiasm and respect all over the world. Since there are around three hundred and thirty million deities in the Hindu faith, it is a polytheistic religion. Each deity is and has its meaning related to an act or ritual. There is a tradition in Nepal of worshipping a family deity (Kul devata) along with the other deities. People select the deities they want to worship everyday. By offering candy, flowers and tika, the individuals worship the deities. They also sing prayers, also known as Bhajans, dedicated to impressing particular gods.

Buddhism

It is the second prevalent religion, being the birthplace of the greatest messenger of Buddhism, Gautam Buddha. During the rule of the 7th Kirat King Jitedasti, Buddhism was first introduced by Gautam Buddha in Nepal.During the reign of King Sthunko, it spread further when the Indian emperor ordered the building of the Ashoka Pillars in Lumbini, Buddha's birthplace.

Islam

Established by Muhammad, also known as God's messenger, Islam is the world's second-largest religion. Islam occurs in limited numbers in Nepal, but they are very committed to their faith. During the 17th century, northern Indians introduced the Islamic faith to Nepal. Although it is possible to date the first Muslims to reach Nepal back to the 15th century when Kashmiri merchants came to trade here. During this time, some Afghans and Persians also served as weapons trainers or manufacturers, and it is believed during that period that Islamic religion spread.

Kiratism

Kiratism was first adopted by the people of the Kirat dynasty and is a religion practiced in Nepal, India and Tibet. It was known as Nepal's principal religion. Kirati people worshiped nature and their ancestors. The Kirat ethnic community living in the hilly and mountain regions of Nepal practises Kiratism. Their holibook is "Mundhum". Rai, Sunwar, Limbu and Yakkha comprise the Kirats.
